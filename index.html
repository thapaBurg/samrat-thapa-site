F-1 Visa Guide

The Interview Is Your One Shot. Be Ready.

Rejection rates for Nepali F-1 applicants have been rising. Here is what separates approvals from denials — and how Weber Academy helps you prepare.

Show Strong Ties to Nepal

The consular officer must believe you will return after graduation. Prepare evidence of family, career plans, property, or community roots — and be ready to articulate them clearly.

Know Your University & Program Inside Out

Why this school? Why this major? Why the USA? Vague, generic answers raise red flags. Be specific, credible, and confident about every detail of your academic plan.

Demonstrate Genuine Financial Sufficiency

You must show you or your sponsor can authentically afford your studies without unauthorized work. Bank statements must be genuine and you must be able to explain them.

Communicate Clearly in English

You don't need perfect English — you need to communicate calmly and clearly. Practice until your answers feel natural and confident, not memorized and robotic.

Prepare for Unexpected Questions

Officers sometimes ask off-script questions to test authenticity. Know your application inside-out so nothing catches you off guard. Mock interviews are essential.

Bring Complete, Organized Documents

Have your I-20, acceptance letter, financial documents, passport, DS-160 confirmation, SEVIS fee receipt, and supporting evidence neatly organized before your appointment.

⚠️ Don't Walk Into Your Interview Unprepared

📊 Rising rejection rates among Nepali F-1 applicants mean thorough preparation is no longer optional — it's essential. You get one shot at the consulate window.

Funding Your Education

Scholarships for International Students

Funding a US education is possible — but it requires strategy, early action, and knowing where to look. Here are the main categories of funding available to Nepali students.

Fulbright Foreign Student Program
Full funding — tuition, living, travel

The most prestigious US government scholarship. Covers full tuition, living expenses, health insurance, and airfare. Extremely competitive — apply through the US Embassy in Kathmandu.

📅 Deadline: Usually February–March each year
University Merit Scholarships
$2,000 – $25,000 / year

Most US universities offer merit scholarships to international students based on academic achievement. Apply early — many are awarded automatically at admission. Dr. Thapa's partner schools offer strong merit aid.

📅 Varies by university — apply with admission
Research / Teaching Assistantships
Tuition waiver + stipend

Graduate students can earn a full tuition waiver plus a monthly stipend (typically $1,200–$2,000/month) by working as a research or teaching assistant. Highly competitive — strong academics and research experience required.

📅 Apply when applying for graduate admission
EducationUSA Opportunity Funds
Application fee waivers + support

For high-achieving Nepali students who lack financial resources. EducationUSA/USEF Nepal provides fee waivers, SAT prep, essay coaching, and mentorship for applying to US universities. Dr. Thapa is a regular USEF speaker.

📅 Applications open annually — check usef.org.np
AAUW International Fellowships
$18,000 – $30,000

Available to women who are not US citizens pursuing full-time graduate or postdoctoral study in the USA. Strong emphasis on leadership and community impact.

📅 Deadline: November each year
Private & Community Scholarships
$500 – $10,000

Thousands of private scholarships are available through organizations, foundations, and community groups. Websites like Fastweb, Scholarships.com, and Chegg Scholarships list opportunities open to international students.

📅 Year-round — search early and often

Finding scholarships is a strategy, not luck.

Life in the USA

What No One Tells You Before You Arrive

Succeeding in America is about more than academics. Here's the practical knowledge every international student needs — from day one to graduation.

🏠
Finding Housing

Take on-campus housing your first year — it's the easiest way to settle in and make friends. For off-campus, learn how to read lease agreements, understand utilities, and find trustworthy roommates. Always see a place in person before signing.

💳
Banking & Building Credit

Open a US bank account on arrival (Chase, Bank of America, or your local credit union). Apply for a secured credit card immediately to start building US credit history — it matters for future housing, phone plans, and careers.

📚
US Academic Culture

American professors expect class participation, critical thinking, and you to visit office hours. Don't wait until you're struggling — build relationships with faculty early. They write your recommendation letters and open doors.

🚗
Transportation & Driver's License

In many US cities and suburbs, a car is essential. Get an International Driving Permit before leaving Nepal. Apply for a state driver's license within your first few months — requirements vary by state.

💼
CPT & OPT Work Authorization

F-1 students can work through Curricular Practical Training (CPT) during school and Optional Practical Training (OPT) after graduation. Understanding these rules early helps you plan internships and post-graduation career paths.

Weber Academy helps with internship placements →

📱
Phone & SIM Cards

Get a prepaid SIM on arrival — T-Mobile and AT&T have good options. Once you have a US bank account, switch to a postpaid plan. Google Fi and Mint Mobile are budget-friendly choices popular with international students.

🏥
Health Insurance

Most universities require international students to enroll in the university health plan. Understand your coverage before you need it. On-campus student health centers are usually free or very affordable for enrolled students.

🧠
Mental Health & Culture Shock

Homesickness, academic pressure, and culture shock are real and very common. Every university offers free counseling services — use them without shame. Join the Nepali Student Association (NSA) to stay connected with your community.

💰
Filing US Taxes

Every F-1 student must file a US tax return by April 15 — even if you earned no income. Use Sprintax (built for international students) or your university's free VITA tax preparation service to file correctly and stay compliant.
